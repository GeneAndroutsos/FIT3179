Australian Climate Visualisations (1975 – 2019)

This dashboard explores how Australia’s climate has changed over the past four decades, drawing on temperature data from 112 official ACORN-SAT weather stations nationwide.

Through interactive visualisations, we uncover long-term trends in maximum temperatures, the effects of elevation and geography, and the growing signs of a warming continent.

A National View of the Australian Climate

Average Maximum Yearly Temperature Across Australia

This interactive map displays the average maximum yearly temperatures across all ACORN-SAT stations. Generally, weather stations in northern Australian experience hotter conditions, with colder conditions further south of the country.

Use the slider to explore how temperature distribution has changed from 1975 to 2019 across different regions of Australia.

How the Yearly National Average Temperature Compares to the Long-Term Mean

This anomaly chart visualises deviations from Australia’s long-term average temperature. While the average maximum temperature in Australia from 1975 to 2019 has been 25 degrees celsius, the country has experienced gradually increasing maximum temperatures.

Most recently, we experienced a nationwide high in 2019 at 29 degrees celsius, 3.5 degrees above the nation average.

Drilling Down on States and Territories

Average Maximum Temperature Heatmap by State and Year

This heatmap shows how each state’s average maximum temperature has evolved over time. The colour gradient highlights warming trends and regional differences across Australia. The Northern Territory and Queensland experience the highest average maximum temperatures, while Tasmania and the ACT experience the lowest.

Average Maximum Temperature Trends by State and Territory

This line chart compares temperature trends across states and territories. It demonstrates that the Northern Territory exhibits the highest temperatures over the past few decades, while Tasmania shows the lowest.

Select a specific state to highlight it and observe its trend relative to others across Australia’s diverse climates.

Observing Temperatures by Stations

Relationship Between Elevation and Average Maximum Temperature

This scatterplot shows how elevation influences temperature. Higher-altitude weather stations generally record lower average maximum temperatures, illustrating an inverse relationship between the two variables.

"Butlers Gorge" highlights an example of a station with a high elevation (667m) and relatively low average max temperature (14 degrees celsius).

Yearly Temperature Trends by Weather Station

The histogram allows us to determine any trends between average max temperature and weather stations, highlighting yearly variations in average maximum temperature.

Most weather stations have experienced an increasing maximum temperature over the past 4 decades.
